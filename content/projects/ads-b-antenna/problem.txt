At the beginning of my freshman year at UW, my dorm had a spectacular elevated
view of downtown Seattle and Mt. Rainier. While this was exciting, what really got me pumped was that our building was situated 
directly below the in-bound flight path for all arrivals at Sea-Tac airport. Plane spotting is one of my favorite things to do, so I frequently used the flight
tracker app, FlightRadar24, to guess-and-check my spotting skills. But as a broke college student with no desire to pay for 
a premium subscription, I was annoyingly bombarded with ads and minimal data on aircraft specs. I wanted a way to track planes
24/7 that was low-cost, accurate, and unique to my convienient location.
