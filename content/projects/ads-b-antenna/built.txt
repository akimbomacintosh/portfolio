To accomplish my goal, I constructed an ADS-B (Aircraft Dependent Surveillance Broadcast) Antenna to track aircraft locally.
These antennas, when designed properly, pick up the 1090MHz frequency band, which is what all commercial, private (and sometimes military)
aircraft broadcast when in-flight. This signal broadcasts a specific type of data when decoded reveals information such as aircraft type, airline,
destination, origin, speed, altitude, and more. What surprised me was the 1090MHz band was broadcasted for truly anyone to read, just like any other radio frequency
you could tune to on a radio. I discovered this kind of RF antenna after researching how FlightRadar24 acquires their data. They commonly enhance their tracking model
with independent antennas scattered around the globe. It was here that I learned that anyone willing to send their antenna data to FlightRadar24
receives a free business tier subscription to their platform, which motivated me even more to complete the project.
